What are the Miami Heat and Charlotte Hornets?

The Miami Heat and Charlotte Hornets are professional basketball teams based in the Eastern Conference of the National Basketball Association (NBA). The Miami Heat, founded in 1988, represent Miami, Florida, and compete in the Southeast Division. The Charlotte Hornets, established in 1988 and re-established in 2004, represent Charlotte, North Carolina, also in the Southeast Division. Both teams are members of the NBA, a major professional sports league.

Examples of NBA teams include the Los Angeles Lakers and Boston Celtics. The Heat's home arena is the Kaseya Center, while the Hornets play at the Spectrum Center. These franchises comprise 15-player rosters, coaching staffs, and front office personnel.
